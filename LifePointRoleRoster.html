Hospital Sponsor The Hospital Sponsor is a senior executive within the hospital or healthcare organization who plays a pivotal role in the EHR changeover process. They are responsible for providing overall strategic direction and leadership for the implementation project. The Hospital Sponsor ensures that the project aligns with the organization's mission, goals, and budgetary constraints. They are often the decision-makers and advocate for the project at the highest levels of the organization.
Hospital Clinical Informatics Director (CI) The Hospital Clinical Informatics Director is a healthcare professional with expertise in clinical practices and information technology. They bridge healthcare providers (such as doctors and nurses) and the EHR system. Their role includes defining and optimizing clinical workflows within the EHR, ensuring that the system supports patient care effectively, and promoting best practices in using the EHR to improve patient outcomes.
Information Services Director (ISD) The Information Services Director, also known as the IT Director or Chief Information Officer (CIO), is responsible for the technical aspects of the EHR implementation. They oversee the IT infrastructure, hardware, software, and data security related to the EHR system. They collaborate with other stakeholders to ensure that the EHR aligns with the organization's IT strategy and complies with regulatory requirements.
Human Resources (HR) The Human Resources (HR) department plays a crucial role in EHR changeover by managing the human capital aspects of the project. HR is responsible for recruiting and training staff, including end-users and support personnel, who will be involved in the EHR implementation. They also handle personnel-related issues, such as job roles, responsibilities, and compliance with workforce policies.
Super User Super Users are key individuals selected from the healthcare organization who receive specialized training on the new EHR system. They act as internal experts and champions of the system during and after the implementation. Super Users assist their colleagues in using the EHR, troubleshoot issues, and provide feedback to the project team. They are critical in ensuring a smooth transition to the new EHR.
